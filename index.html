About

Research

I am a member of the Topology and Data Science research groups and am part of the Centre for Topological Data Analysis. My main interests are in computational algebraic topology using a categorical approach, computational neuroscience, network analysis and applied knot theory.

I've taken the following graduate courses during my DPhil: Homological Algebra, Category Theory, Differentiable Manifolds, Probability & Statistics for Network Analysis and Machine Learning.

Below is a brief summary of the research projects I have worked on so far.

A generalised clock theorem

Joint with Daniele Celoria

We obtain a simple and complete characterisation of which matchings on the Tait graph of a knot diagram induce a discrete Morse function on the 2-sphere and simultaneously generalise Kauffman's Clock Theorem and Kenyon-Propp-Wilson’s correspondence.

Preprint

Filtered matchings and simplicial complexes

Joint with Daniele Celoria

We associate to any simplicial complex a filtration, starting from the discrete Morse complex and finishing at the matching complex. We define some homology theories and provide computations to help to understand these complex objects.

Preprint

Topological comparison of graph metrics

Joint with Ana Garcia-Pulido, Kathryn Hess, Erika Roldan, Jane Tan, Katharine Turner & Bei Wang

Motivated by modelling the brain as a digraph, we investigate whether standard digraph metrics capture topological information about the associated directed flag complex, for various random digraph models.

Preprint in preparation.
